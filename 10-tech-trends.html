10 Global Tech Trends

  • Blockchain Technology

  • Quantum Computing

  • Cybersecurity
  • Autonomous Vehicles
  • Cloud Computing

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is considered a technology in the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) impacting various industries worldwide. AI involves replicating intelligence in machines to enable them to think and learn independently. AI encompasses an array of technologies, such as machine learning and natural language processing, among others. These technologies can tackle issues by leveraging datasets for learning (Russell & Norvig, 2021). In South Africa's setting, artificial intelligence plays a role in advancing fields such as healthcare, agriculture, and finance, bolstering economic development and addressing socioeconomic obstacles. The integration of AI in South Africa encounters obstacles due to insufficiencies in infrastructure and expertise shortages ( Mothibi, 2021 ).

Blockchain Technology

Blockchain technology can radically alter all existing business models, reduce the cost of financial services, increase financial inclusion, and perhaps even result in a fairer distribution of wealth (Schinckus, 2020). Blockchain technology seems to be a good option for informing all parties engaged in the agri-food supply chain because of its technical and governance qualities (Mavilia & Pisani, 2022). Ehsani claims that the idea of a Sovereign or Private Ledger run by a central bank (the South African Reserve Bank) and different commercial banks is the first use of blockchain technology that has the potential to drastically alter how banks conduct business with one another and with their clients.

5G Networks

Future 5G wireless communications are anticipated to provide very high data rates (typically in the Gbps range), very low latency, a multiplicity of base station capacity increases, and a notable improvement in users' perceived quality of service (QoS) compared to current 4G LTE networks (Agiwal et al., 2016). Due to its capacity to connect to anything, the Internet of Things (IoT) is one of the 5G enabling technologies that allow many to coexist (Javaid et al., 2018). The massive number of connected devices in a given area that 5G's support for higher device density makes feasible is essential for the increasing number of linked devices in industrial applications and smart cities.

Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) concept entails linking objects to the internet for data exchange. This innovation has transformed how devices interact and manage tasks efficiently (referencing Gubbi et al., 2020). In South Africa, specifically, IoT shows promise in improving sectors like agriculture through sensor applications for tracking soil conditions, water consumption levels, and crop well-being (citing Van der Merwe, 2021). The implementation of IoT in South Africa faces challenges due to connectivity issues in areas where the essential infrastructure is lacking for its deployment.

Quantum Computing

Based on the fundamental ideas of quantum physics, quantum computing is a new paradigm in computing that makes calculations previously unattainable with classical computers possible. Although technology is still in its early stages, quantum computing has the potential to fundamentally transform artificial intelligence, drugs, and cryptography in ways that are unimaginable with current technologies. Quantum computing research in South Africa is considered at its foundation stages and might potentially impact finance, telecommunications, and mining. According to Molokoane & Mntambo 2021, this country needs to be developed in terms of quantum talent and investment in quantum infrastructure.

Edge Computing

Since data is being generated at the network's edge more and more, processing it there would be more effective (Shi et al., 2016). Research has shown that when data is generated at the network's edge, cloud computing may not necessarily be the most effective method for processing it. Because the fundamental premise of edge computing is to move computation closer to the data source, this is necessary (Cao et al., 2020). So, doing this will minimize latency and boost bandwidth between the networks.

Cybersecurity

With the acceleration of digital transformation in South Africa, cybersecurity has grown in importance. Cybersecurity protects systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks aimed at disabling them and stealing sensitive information or impairing services (NIST, 2021). In South Africa, the frequency of cyberattacks has increased tremendously since most of these attacks target public and private institutions (Moyo, 2020). While efforts are considered to prepare the country to be secure in terms of cybersecurity, there is still a challenge to the skills gap and outdated infrastructure that makes South Africa vulnerable to advanced cyber threats.

Autonomous Vehicles

The most significant barrier to the development and widespread application of autonomous vehicles (AVs), even though they are believed to be the finest solution for automotive engineering in the future, is safety (Wang et al., 2020). It is concluded that South Africa may not be ready for autonomous car legislation and that the upgrade of current infrastructure and the maintenance thereof may be necessary and expensive for the country (Jacobs, 2020). This should not deter South Africa from initiating the legislative process in this regard, as it might take some time for lawmakers to create legislation.

Cloud Computing

Cloud computing is generally considered to be the provision of storage, databases, servers, networking, software, and other types of computing via the internet, or "the cloud." The technology enables organizations to access and store data without depending on local servers, thus offering scalability, flexibility, and cost-efficiency (Armbrust et al., 2020). In general, cloud computing has been transforming industries globally through faster application deployment by companies in a way that does not require massive physical infrastructure to manage significant volumes of data. Cloud computing has been considered the much-needed catalyst for digital transformation in South Africa, especially within financial services, education, and healthcare. Its adoption helped businesses in South Africa streamline their operations and improve customer experience, along with other business processes (Pillay & Maharaj, 2021). Nevertheless, data privacy concerns, compliance with regulations, and various high-speed internet shortcomings within rural areas bring up many barriers to broader application. Further, the dependence on foreign cloud service providers raises issues of data sovereignty; therefore, there is a need for data centers and South Africa-specific cloud services.

Virtual/Augmented Reality (VR/AR)

Virtual and augmented reality allows users to become "immersed" in a digital environment or superimpose digital information on the physical world, respectively. These technologies' applications range from education and healthcare to gaming (Azuma, 2020). In South Africa, there is a gradual adoption of VR and AR in tourism and educational institutions in order to improve the learning experiences and come up with new ideas for marketing. According to Zondo & Radebe (2021), poor adoption in the country can be attributed to high costs from both technologies and lack of technical expertise.
