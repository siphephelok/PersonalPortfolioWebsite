South Africa and the 4IR/5IR

An Academic Perspective on SA's Position in the Fourth and Fifth Industrial Revolutions

Current Position Benefits of 4IR/5IR Challenges Necessary Changes

South Africa's Current Position in 4IR and 5IR (2024)

With its economy still based on farming, mining, and the unorganized sector, high unemployment rates, and a large population lacking advanced and frequently fundamental skills, South Africa is not a popular destination for 4IR (Sutherland, 2020). Other studies have questioned South Africa's preparedness for the Fourth Industrial Revolution. (Levin, 2018) Declare that Industry 4.0 readiness is still needed in South Africa. They also argue that government leadership and coordination are lacking, leading to a fragmentation of government agencies, programs, and funding sources despite the convergence of technologies.

It has identified eight barriers to effectively leveraging prospects in the context of the fourth industrial revolution (Mtotywa et al., 2022). These include the high cost of data, low economic inclusion, a lack of digital culture, youth literacy and skill deficiencies, the manual versus automated labor conflict, a high rate of crime, a lack of entrepreneurial inclination, and insufficient privacy and security in the digital and social media spheres.

Potential Benefits of 4IR and 5IR for South Africa

  • Women are prioritized, and there is deep multilevel cooperation between people and machines, as well as inclusivity and innovation goals (RAMPITSANG, 2023).
  • Enhanced healthcare systems via IoT and AI technologies, improving patient care and data management
  • Computers may now be programmed to perform specific jobs by processing vast volumes of data and finding patterns in the data thanks to the application of new technologies (Kayembe & Nel, 2019).
  • Advancements in education, providing greater access to digital learning and bridging the skills gap
  • Human-technology integration in 5IR could promote inclusive growth, addressing socio-economic challenges

Challenges South Africa Faces to Capitalize on 4IR and 5IR

  • Inequality in access to technology between urban and rural areas
  • Skills gap in STEM fields, limiting the workforce's ability to adapt to new technologies
  • Lack of infrastructure to support widespread adoption of digital technologies
  • Regulatory challenges and slow policy reforms that delay technological progress
  • The fourth industrial revolution "may indeed have the potential to 'robotize' humanity and thus deprive us of our heart and soul," according to (Manda & Ben Dhaou, 2019).

Necessary Changes for South Africa to Leverage 4IR and 5IR Opportunities

  • Investing in infrastructure development to support digital transformation, especially in rural areas
  • Implementing educational reforms to prioritize STEM subjects and digital literacy from a young age
  • Encouraging public-private partnerships to drive innovation and fund R&D in emerging technologies
  • Developing a regulatory framework that fosters innovation while protecting society from technological risks
  • Promoting digital inclusion to ensure that all citizens, regardless of location or socio-economic status, benefit from 4IR and 5IR advancements
