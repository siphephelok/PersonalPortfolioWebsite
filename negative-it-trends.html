Negative Impacts of Tech Trends

Negative Impacts of 3 Selected Tech Trends

1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is feared to replace human jobs and pose ethical challenges, including bias and surveillance. According to Frey and Osborne (2017), automation through AI may displace many jobs, particularly those in manufacturing and retail. Ethical issues also arise from biased algorithms, as noted by Barocas, Hardt, and Narayanan (2019), which amplify existing societal inequalities. Additionally, AI-powered mass surveillance raises concerns about privacy violations and misuse for authoritarian control (Zuboff, 2019).

2. Blockchain Technology

Blockchain faces environmental concerns due to its energy-intensive mining processes. Krause and Tolaymat (2018) point out that cryptocurrency mining consumes vast amounts of electricity, contributing to significant carbon emissions. This has led to debates on the sustainability of blockchain applications beyond cryptocurrency. Although alternatives like Proof of Stake (PoS) are being explored, blockchain's environmental footprint remains a key challenge (Sedlmeir et al., 2020).

3. 5G Networks

There are concerns about privacy and health risks as 5G rolls out worldwide. Porambage et al. (2018) highlight that the proliferation of connected devices in 5G networks increases the risk of cyberattacks and data breaches. Moreover, some communities express worries about potential health risks from radiofrequency radiation, although the WHO (2020) maintains there is no conclusive evidence to support these claims. Calls for further research continue as 5G expands globally (Russell, 2018).
